The Ceiling Problem

AI has a hard limit.
It's not a bug.

In 2025, Vishal Sikka — former CTO of SAP, CEO of Infosys, Stanford PhD who studied under John McCarthy (the man who coined "Artificial Intelligence") — published a paper proving it formally.

"For any task whose verification complexity exceeds what one forward pass can compute — the model cannot tell a good output from a bad one."

— Sikka & Sikka, Hallucination Stations (2025)

Apple's research team confirmed it independently. Frontier reasoning models like o3 don't just perform worse at high complexity — their accuracy drops to zero. And they reduce reasoning effort right when the problem gets hardest.

This is why autonomous AI businesses keep failing. This is why agents hallucinate at the worst possible moment. This is the ceiling everyone is pretending doesn't exist.

Four steps.
No ceiling.

SRI doesn't raise the ceiling. It redesigns the workflow so you never reach it. Every complex goal flows through four layers — each one keeping AI operating in the zone where it is provably reliable.

01 — ⬡

Decompose

Every goal is broken into atomic steps, each budgeted to stay below the complexity threshold. No single AI call ever handles more than it can reliably process.

Core Engine
02 — ◎

Simulate

Before acting, MiroFish spawns thousands of independent agents to simulate the consequence. Catch strategic errors before they happen in the real world.

MiroFish
03 — ◻

Verify

Deterministic checks on every output before the next step runs. The verifier enforces the contract between steps — no hallucination compounds.

Verifier
04 — △

Gate

Human approval before high-stakes actions. Not a limitation — the feature that makes SRI safe to run on real operations. Automate the routine. Escalate the important.

Human Gate
05 — ∞

Learn

Every approved workflow is written as a persistent skill via Hermes. The system compounds. It never re-learns what it already knows. Intelligence that grows.

Hermes
The Stack
⬡

MiroFish

Swarm intelligence engine that spawns thousands of AI agents with independent personalities and memory. SRI uses it not to predict the future — but to simulate the consequence of a single decision before taking it.

Simulation Layer
∞

Hermes Agent

Persistent skill-based AI agent by Nous Research. Every problem solved becomes a skill document stored permanently. In SRI, every approved workflow becomes a Hermes skill — the system gets smarter every time it succeeds.

Skill Memory
◻

Human Gate

The layer everyone else removed. SRI builds it in as a first-class primitive. Every major AI framework races toward zero human involvement. The Sikka math says that's why they fail. The gate is not a bottleneck — it's the guarantee.

Trust Layer
Origin

Built by a student who refused to accept the ceiling.

SRI began with a video. A student in India watched Vishal Sikka explain a 60-year-old theorem that proved why every AI agent eventually hallucinates — and immediately asked: can we solve this?

One hour later, the repo existed. Built with AI tools, directed by human insight. The same way Tony Stark built the suit.

The idea is simple: if the ceiling exists per AI call, never make a call that hits it. Decompose every goal until every piece fits. Verify every output. Let humans approve what matters. Never re-learn what you already know.
